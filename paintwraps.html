Disclaimer and Advice:

Posted by Ryan | Paint & Wraps

These are just my opinions and thoughts and even though that's been the idea across the site for the most part - I know people have different opinions about this subject so you are welcome to respectfully discuss those opinions in the Community Posts page, but any post that is not respectful and cordial will be removed.

In regards to paintjobs and wraps: I think the best way to decide between getting a paintjob or a wrap for your car or bike is just to determine the value for price as well as how often you believe you might try another color on your build. Meaning, maybe you have a project build and use it for your social media - maybe it is a good idea to wrap it so that you can change the color or revert it to the factory paint as you please to help your social media grow. Maybe you just never really liked or grew tired of the paintjob your vehicle came from factory with and believe just changing it completely would help you enjoy and appreciate the vehicle more which means a paintjob would be better for you. Regardless of the choice you make, both allow an individual to express themselves through their automotive build and as long as you are carefully weighing your options, making the decision to change your car's appearance with this mod hopefully satiates your desire while helping you enjoy or appreciate your vehicle more.

Car Paint and Wrap Inspiration:

Posted by Ryan | Car Paint & Wraps

Yes, my favorite color is orange as you can imagine, but you can't lie when it comes to how good this Mercedes-Benz AMG GT looks in a Lava Orange wrap. I think it really pops and brings eyes to the car and all it's distinct features, but its not an extreme eyesore on the road. I would argue this wrap is a great middleground between the two extremes and that is why I am interested in wrapping my car the same color later down the line.

Motorcycle Paint and Wrap Inspiration:

Posted by Ryan | Motorcycle Paint & Wraps

This is a wrap you don't see too often, but it was just too good for me to not feature and talk about. The wrap is pretty cool on it's own with how well the gold japanese lettering and purple mesh on this Yamaha, but what takes it to the next level is the reference it is making. For those who don't realize it, this wrap is based off of the robot "Noisy Boy" from the movie Real Steel which I enjoyed from childhood and have enjoyed whenever I end up rewatching it every once in awhile. Furthermore, referencing a japanese robot from the movie and using the wrap on a japanese manufactured bike is the cherry on it all. I appreciate thought behind design like this and would love to implement something similar to my own bike when I eventually get one.
